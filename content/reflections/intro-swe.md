Software engineering is something I stumbled upon during my internships. My core area when I was studying was in AI and Analytics. However, I knew that was just one part of Computing, and I wanted to explore the different areas and give all of them a try. I did cyber security software engineering, analytics, and devops internships. This exposure gave me the confidence that software engineering was something I enjoyed.

I landed my first job in software engineering, and built and maintained systems that are scalable and reliable. I was involved in the end-to-end product design of many deliverables: from understanding the core problem, to solutioning it and contributing to architectural designs, to actually building the solutions. These enhanced my skills in architecting, in understanding and evaluating cost-benefit analysis when deciding on a solution, and of course as a developer. I got to use tools like Spark, AWS, Databricks, Java, Python, Kafka, and many more.

I built an end-to-end solution to allow application teams to manage the inventory of their ingested datasets, ensuring the critical information needed to set a benchmark for data-quality validations is available. This application now surfaces over 800 datasets across 4 teams. 

In the more recent years, with the evolution of AI, I was able to experience AI-assisted development, and it really changed my view on what development work will be like in the future.
