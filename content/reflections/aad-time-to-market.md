I recently started working on a project with a group of 3 developers, to convert a local minimum viable product (MVP) into a deliverable product in 5 months. We started breaking down tasks, creating JIRA boards and estimated a delivery of 4 months, given a 15 hours per week commitment per developer. This was considered a high commitment work with low buffer for busy work schedules for all of us. We weren't certain if we could deliver by then. Then, something hit me. I thought - if I was using AI-assisted development at work, why can't I do the same for this project? 

To convince my peers that this was the way to go for this project, I took the leap of faith and started developing with the help of AI. I started with prompt engineering, improving and optimizing how I want the application to be, giving "clear" instructions to the AI. Then moved on to creating a whole agent ecosystem, cause why not? If I can ask an agent to coordinate the subagents, all I need to do was to manage the main coordinater agent. So then I went to that route and started seeing huge progress in the work. As i continued through the night, I managed to get the monolitic repository broken down into microservices. Our code base was adhering to certain industry standards. I looked at the time and was genuinely impressed by what AI-assited development can produce. In just 7 hours, I managed to complete half the work planned. This is approximately 80 hours of work condensed into just 7 hours. Needless to say, they were definitely convinced and hopped on the bandwagon. 

While I have to admit, this time savings is not proportionate (80h does not equals to 7h of development), this creates huge time savings. It really depends on many factors like the level of understanding it has on the repository, the amount of reasoning it has to perform. Notwithstanding, if we have any technical deliverables, such as legacy code upgrade or refactoring, quick proof of concepts, or picking up a new repository for development, leveraging AI-assistants will definitely boost a developer's productivity. 

Frankly, I spent the most time to setup the agentic ecosystem (loop). It requires a lot of research, trial and error, and when faced with an unexpected scenario, we had to go back to brainstormining why something broke and how to fix it. This is something that I will address as part of my agent harness reflection.
