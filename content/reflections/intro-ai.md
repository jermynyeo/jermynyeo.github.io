Artificial Intelligence (AI) and analytics. This is something that I have always been interested in since my first year of University. I took on a major in Business Analytics, and through my coursework, I learnt about Data Visualization, Text Mining, Machine Learning, and Artificial Intelligence. The most interesting part of this field is that it was continuously evolving. I vividly recall my Professor teaching us in class that text summarization is an unsolved problem, and here we are, in 2026, with text summarization as one of the easiest problems to solve.

My two most memorable projects in school were building a social network to help clients understand their social standing in the market, and feature engineering for fake news detection. Can you believe it? Fake news detection and classification was a big thing back then! Now, these are easily answered by large language models (LLMs).

Unfortunately, due to a packed schedule, I wasn't able to continue learning much in this area. But needless to say, I still kept in touch with the new technologies and papers (once in a while) about the newest releases in the industry. I watched the field move from prompt engineering being THE thing, to few-shot prompting to improve the context window of an LLM chatbot, and now to agents integrating themselves into the software development lifecycle (SDLC), delivering products at high speed.

Seeing how the space of AI evolved through the years, I'm super excited to see how it integrates with software engineering and beyond! I am currently looking into certifying myself, and have started building some applications with AI assistance.
